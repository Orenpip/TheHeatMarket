     1вҶ’# COMPASS вҖ” Product Design Requirements (PDR)
     2вҶ’## A Student Operating System for Self-Authorship
     3вҶ’
     4вҶ’---
     5вҶ’
     6вҶ’## 1. PRODUCT VISION
     7вҶ’
     8вҶ’### 1.1 One-Line Summary
     9вҶ’Compass is a longitudinal student operating system that turns raw high school experience into directional clarity вҖ” not by telling students what to do, but by making their own signals visible and expanding their awareness of what's possible.
    10вҶ’
    11вҶ’### 1.2 Core Philosophy
    12вҶ’The product is built on five principles derived from real student experience:
    13вҶ’
    14вҶ’1. **Neuroplasticity as foundation** вҖ” Identity is a variable, not a constant. The system treats students as becoming, not being.
    15вҶ’2. **Read, apply, compound** вҖ” Curiosity without application is trivia. The system connects intellectual interest to real-world experimentation.
    16вҶ’3. **Discipline shapes identity** вҖ” Character formation (focus, endurance, frustration tolerance) matters as much as intellectual preference.
    17вҶ’4. **Intrinsic motivation over external validation** вҖ” The system never optimizes for prestige. It optimizes for engagement and growth.
    18вҶ’5. **Community as accelerant** вҖ” No one keeps you accountable like a peer. The system connects students in exploration, not competition.
    19вҶ’
    20вҶ’### 1.3 The Problem
    21вҶ’High school students suffer from **directional blindness**, not lack of ability. They generate signals constantly (books they read, classes they enjoy, activities they choose) but no system helps them see the pattern. By the time they discover career paths, major combinations, and industry pipelines, years have been wasted in reactive prestige-chasing.
    22вҶ’
    23вҶ’Current tools (Naviance, Scoir, CollegeBoard) extract information from students for institutional purposes. Compass inverts this: everything belongs to the student first.
    24вҶ’
    25вҶ’### 1.4 The Solution
    26вҶ’A **reflection engine** вҖ” not a direction engine. It functions as a mirror with memory:
    27вҶ’- Tracks what students are actually doing and thinking
    28вҶ’- Surfaces patterns they don't consciously see
    29вҶ’- Expands their awareness of career paths, programs, and opportunities
    30вҶ’- Never prescribes direction вҖ” only reveals the map
    31вҶ’
    32вҶ’### 1.5 What Compass Is NOT
    33вҶ’- Not an admissions optimizer
    34вҶ’- Not a career quiz
    35вҶ’- Not an AI essay writer
    36вҶ’- Not a college ranking tool
    37вҶ’- Not a ChatGPT wrapper
    38вҶ’- Not a surveillance tool for schools or parents
    39вҶ’
    40вҶ’---
    41вҶ’
    42вҶ’## 2. USER PERSONAS
    43вҶ’
    44вҶ’### 2.1 Primary: The Intense Drifter (Student)
    45вҶ’- High school student (grades 9вҖ“12), ages 14вҖ“18
    46вҶ’- Motivated and hardworking but lacks directional clarity
    47вҶ’- Doesn't know what industries exist, what majors combine into, or what career architectures look like
    48вҶ’- Generates strong signals through their choices but can't see the pattern
    49вҶ’- Needs map visibility, not motivation
    50вҶ’
    51вҶ’### 2.2 Secondary: The Overwhelmed Counselor
    52вҶ’- School guidance counselor managing 300вҖ“500+ students
    53вҶ’- Meets each student 1-3 times per year
    54вҶ’- Knows almost nothing about most students' evolving interests
    55вҶ’- Needs a synthesized dashboard showing each student's trajectory
    56вҶ’- Does NOT need raw data вҖ” needs patterns and gaps
    57вҶ’
    58вҶ’### 2.3 Tertiary: The Anxious Parent (Future Phase)
    59вҶ’- Wants reassurance their child is on track
    60вҶ’- Currently spends money on admissions consultants out of fear
    61вҶ’- Needs a "peace of mind" view вҖ” not surveillance
    62вҶ’- Only sees what the student explicitly shares
    63вҶ’

    66вҶ’## 3. CORE FEATURES
    67вҶ’
    68вҶ’### 3.1 Monthly Structured Reflection (School Integration)
    69вҶ’The primary data input mechanism. Designed to be used during existing advisory/homeroom periods (~20 minutes/month).
    70вҶ’
    71вҶ’**Session Flow:**
    72вҶ’1. **Check-in pulse** вҖ” Quick emotional/energy state (not a mood tracker вҖ” a context setter)
    73вҶ’2. **Activity log update** вҖ” What have you been doing this month? Classes, clubs, books, projects, hobbies, jobs, volunteer work. Semi-structured input with smart suggestions based on prior entries.
    74вҶ’3. **Reflection prompts** вҖ” 2-3 AI-generated questions personalized to the student's trajectory. These are the core product differentiator. Examples:
    75вҶ’   - "You've spent time on both debate and coding this semester. What do you think connects those two interests?"
    76вҶ’   - "Last month you said you were curious about how businesses work. Have you explored that further, or has your curiosity shifted?"
    77вҶ’   - "You mentioned feeling energized by your chemistry lab. What specifically about it felt alive?"
    78вҶ’4. **Map moment** вҖ” After reflection, the system surfaces 1-2 "did you know?" expansions: career paths, programs, competitions, or industries related to the student's emerging signals.
    79вҶ’5. **Privacy review** вҖ” Student sees a summary of what would be visible to their counselor and toggles individual items on/off before submitting.
    80вҶ’
    81вҶ’**Data captured per session:**
    82вҶ’- Activities (structured: category, time invested, enjoyment level)
    83вҶ’- Free-text reflections
    84вҶ’- Prompted response data
    85вҶ’- Privacy selections
    86вҶ’- Engagement metrics (time spent, depth of responses)
    87вҶ’
    88вҶ’### 3.2 Signal Mining Engine (AI Backend)
    89вҶ’The intelligence layer that identifies patterns across a student's longitudinal data.
    90вҶ’
    91вҶ’**Pattern Detection:**
    92вҶ’- Interest clustering вҖ” groups activities and reflections into emerging theme areas
    93вҶ’- Intensity tracking вҖ” measures depth of engagement vs. surface-level participation
    94вҶ’- Trajectory shifts вҖ” detects when interests evolve, merge, or diverge over time
    95вҶ’- Character signals вҖ” identifies traits like discipline, leadership, creativity, analytical thinking based on activity patterns and reflections
    96вҶ’- Gap detection вҖ” flags areas the student hasn't explored that align with their emerging patterns
    97вҶ’
    98вҶ’**Signal Output (used by other features):**
    99вҶ’- Evolving interest profile (updated monthly)
   100вҶ’- Exploration breadth score (how widely has the student looked?)
   101вҶ’- Depth indicators per interest area
   102вҶ’- Confidence markers (how strong is each signal?)
   103вҶ’- Suggested exploration prompts for the next month
   104вҶ’
   105вҶ’### 3.3 The Map (Career/Education Landscape)
   106вҶ’A living, personalized visualization of possibilities. NOT a static database вҖ” a dynamic landscape that shifts as the student evolves.
   107вҶ’
   108вҶ’**Map Contents:**
   109вҶ’- Career paths and their actual architectures (entry points, progressions, pivots)
   110вҶ’- College major combinations and what they enable
   111вҶ’- Industry pipelines (which schools feed which industries, which majors lead where)
   112вҶ’- Recruiting patterns (target schools, typical paths, timeline)
   113вҶ’- Competitions, research programs, internships, and experiential opportunities
   114вҶ’- Double/triple major viability at specific schools
   115вҶ’- Non-obvious connections (e.g., philosophy + systems engineering + consulting)
   116вҶ’
   117вҶ’**Personalization:**
   118вҶ’- The visible portion of the map highlights areas relevant to the student's signals
   119вҶ’- New regions illuminate as new signals emerge
   120вҶ’- Previously explored areas show "you've been here" indicators
   121вҶ’- Unexplored adjacent areas glow as suggested expansions
   122вҶ’- The map never hides information вҖ” students can always browse the full landscape
   123вҶ’
   124вҶ’**Data Sources (to be built and maintained):**
   125вҶ’- BLS occupational data
   126вҶ’- University program catalogs
   127вҶ’- LinkedIn career path data (aggregated/anonymized)
   128вҶ’- Competition and program databases
   129вҶ’- Recruiting pipeline data from public sources
   130вҶ’- Alumni outcome data where available
   131вҶ’
   132вҶ’### 3.4 Proactive Notification System
   133вҶ’The key differentiator from a ChatGPT wrapper. The system comes to the student with the right question or opportunity at the right time.
   134вҶ’
   135вҶ’**Notification Types:**
   136вҶ’
   137вҶ’1. **Reflection Nudges** вҖ” Between monthly sessions, contextual prompts based on recent activity or upcoming decisions.
   138вҶ’   - "You registered for AP Physics next semester. What drew you to that choice?"
   139вҶ’   - "It's been 3 months since you mentioned interest in writing. Still curious, or has that shifted?"
   140вҶ’
   141вҶ’2. **Opportunity Alerts** вҖ” Timely, relevant opportunities the student wouldn't have found on their own.
   142вҶ’   - "There's a philosophy essay competition with a deadline in 3 weeks. Based on your reflections, you might find the prompt interesting."
   143вҶ’   - "MIT has a free online course on systems thinking that starts next month."
   144вҶ’
   145вҶ’3. **Map Expansions** вҖ” "Did you know?" moments that widen peripheral vision.
   146вҶ’   - "Students who enjoy both math and philosophy often thrive in operations research. Here's what that field looks like."
   147вҶ’   - "UVA has a Systems Engineering program that combines technical and strategic thinking вҖ” and it's a pipeline to consulting."
   148вҶ’
   149вҶ’4. **Peer Connection Prompts** вҖ” Prompts to engage with matched exploration peers (see 3.6).
   150вҶ’
   151вҶ’**Notification Rules:**
   152вҶ’- Maximum 2-3 notifications per week (quality over quantity)
   153вҶ’- Every notification must be either a reflection prompt, a map expansion, or a timely opportunity
   154вҶ’- Notifications are never generic вҖ” always personalized to the student's trajectory
   155вҶ’- Student controls notification frequency and can mute categories
   156вҶ’- No notification should feel like marketing
   157вҶ’
   158вҶ’### 3.5 Counselor Dashboard
   159вҶ’A synthesized view of student trajectories вҖ” NOT raw data.
   160вҶ’
   161вҶ’**What counselors see (by default):**
   162вҶ’- Student's top 3-5 emerging interest areas with confidence levels
   163вҶ’- Exploration breadth score
   164вҶ’- Recent trajectory shifts ("This student's interests have shifted from X toward Y")
   165вҶ’- Engagement level (is the student actively reflecting or disengaged?)
   166вҶ’- Suggested conversation starters for the next 1:1 meeting
   167вҶ’- Flagged gaps ("This student hasn't explored anything related to their strongest signals")
   168вҶ’
   169вҶ’**What counselors do NOT see (unless student shares):**
   170вҶ’- Raw reflection text
   171вҶ’- Specific books read or content consumed
   172вҶ’- Emotional check-in data
   173вҶ’- Any item the student has toggled off
   174вҶ’
   175вҶ’**Counselor Tools:**
   176вҶ’- Filter/sort students by engagement level, interest area, or grade
   177вҶ’- Batch view for identifying students who need attention
   178вҶ’- Meeting prep view вҖ” one-page summary per student before a scheduled meeting
   179вҶ’- Ability to send a personalized reflection prompt to a specific student
   180вҶ’
   181вҶ’### 3.6 Peer Exploration Network
   182вҶ’Lightweight social layer focused on shared exploration, not competition.
   183вҶ’
   184вҶ’**Matching Logic:**
   185вҶ’- Students matched into small groups (3-5) based on similar exploration phase or overlapping interests
   186вҶ’- NOT based on GPA, test scores, or achievement level
   187вҶ’- Can be cross-school (for broader perspective) or within-school (for accountability)
   188вҶ’
   189вҶ’**Interaction Model:**
   190вҶ’- Anonymized or first-name-only profiles
   191вҶ’- Shared reflection prompts that the group responds to
   192вҶ’- "Someone in your group discovered X this month" вҖ” inspiration without comparison
   193вҶ’- No rankings, no metrics visible to peers
   194вҶ’- Opt-in only
   195вҶ’
   196вҶ’### 3.7 Future Simulation (Phase 2)
   197вҶ’Shows possible trajectories based on the student's emerging signals.
   198вҶ’
   199вҶ’**Not prediction вҖ” illustration:**
   200вҶ’- "If you lean into engineering + philosophy, here's what 10 years might look like"
   201вҶ’- Shows 3-5 possible paths based on current signals
   202вҶ’- Each path shows: typical education route, career progression, skills developed, lifestyle characteristics
   203вҶ’- Updates as signals evolve
   204вҶ’- Explicitly framed as possibilities, not prescriptions
   205вҶ’
   206вҶ’### 3.8 Student Privacy Control Center
   207вҶ’The trust architecture is a core product feature, not a compliance checkbox.
   208вҶ’
   209вҶ’**Privacy Principles:**
   210вҶ’- Everything belongs to the student first
   211вҶ’- Default state: private (student opts IN to sharing, not out)
   212вҶ’- Granular control: per-item toggling after every session
   213вҶ’- Audit log: student can always see exactly what their counselor has access to
   214вҶ’- Data portability: student can export their entire history at any time
   215вҶ’- Deletion rights: student can delete any data at any time
   216вҶ’
   217вҶ’**Privacy Dashboard:**
   218вҶ’- Real-time view of what the counselor can currently see
   219вҶ’- Toggle controls for each shared item
   220вҶ’- History of what was shared and when
   221вҶ’- One-click "make everything private" emergency option

   232вҶ’- Transparency as literal design metaphor: frosted glass panels, subtle translucency, light refraction effects
   233вҶ’- High contrast content on soft, luminous backgrounds
   234вҶ’- The premium feel signals to students: "this system takes you seriously"

   367вҶ’### 4.5 Layout Principles
   368вҶ’
   369вҶ’- **Maximum content width:** 720px for reading/reflection views (optimal readability)
   370вҶ’- **Generous whitespace:** minimum 24px between sections, 16px between related elements
   371вҶ’- **Single-column primary flow** on mobile вҖ” no side-by-side cramming
   372вҶ’- **The Map** is the exception вҖ” it's a full-viewport interactive canvas
   373вҶ’- **Progressive disclosure:** show the essential, reveal depth on interaction
   374вҶ’- **No visual clutter:** every element earns its place on screen
   375вҶ’- **Animations:** subtle and purposeful. Entry animations use staggered fade-up (50ms delay between elements). No bouncing, no spinning, no gratuitous motion.
   376вҶ’

   379вҶ’- Monthly sessions may be on school Chromebooks/laptops
   380вҶ’- Touch targets: minimum 44px
   381вҶ’- Bottom navigation bar with 4 tabs: Home, Map, Reflections, Profile
   382вҶ’- Counselor dashboard is desktop-only (or tablet landscape)

   489вҶ’### 5.3 AI Cost Architecture
   490вҶ’
   491вҶ’**Per-student monthly budget target: $2вҖ“5**
   492вҶ’
   493вҶ’**Cost control strategies:**
   494вҶ’
   495вҶ’1. **RAG over full context** вҖ” Never stuff full student history into every call. Store all data in PostgreSQL + vector store. Retrieve only relevant slices per interaction (3вҖ“8k tokens instead of 50вҖ“100k).
   496вҶ’
   497вҶ’2. **Compressed student profile** вҖ” A living summary document (1вҖ“2k tokens) updated monthly after each session. This travels with every interaction as base context.
   498вҶ’
   499вҶ’3. **Tiered model routing:**
   500вҶ’   - Routine notifications and opportunity alerts вҶ’ lightweight model (Claude Haiku tier, ~$0.01/interaction)
   501вҶ’   - Monthly reflection prompt generation вҶ’ mid-tier model (~$0.03/interaction)
   502вҶ’   - Deep signal synthesis and profile updates вҶ’ top-tier model (~$0.05вҖ“0.08/interaction)
   503вҶ’   - Estimated 80% of interactions use the cheap tier
   504вҶ’
   505вҶ’4. **Batch processing** вҖ” Signal mining and profile synthesis run as batch jobs after monthly sessions, not real-time. Opportunity matching runs as a nightly cron against the full student database.
   506вҶ’
   507вҶ’5. **Structured interaction model** вҖ” Prompted reflections with specific questions (not open-ended chat) naturally constrain token usage while producing better data.
   508вҶ’
   509вҶ’**Cost breakdown per student per month (estimated):**
   510вҶ’| Component | Interactions | Cost/Interaction | Monthly Cost |
   511вҶ’|---|---|---|---|
   512вҶ’| Monthly session prompts | 3вҖ“5 | $0.03вҖ“0.05 | $0.15вҖ“0.25 |
   513вҶ’| Signal synthesis (batch) | 1 | $0.08вҖ“0.15 | $0.08вҖ“0.15 |
   514вҶ’| Profile update (batch) | 1 | $0.05вҖ“0.10 | $0.05вҖ“0.10 |
   515вҶ’| Notifications (2вҖ“3/week) | 8вҖ“12 | $0.01вҖ“0.02 | $0.08вҖ“0.24 |
   516вҶ’| Opportunity matching (batch) | 4 (weekly) | $0.02вҖ“0.05 | $0.08вҖ“0.20 |
   517вҶ’| **Subtotal AI costs** | | | **$0.44вҖ“0.94** |

   818вҶ’## 8. AI PROMPT ARCHITECTURE
   819вҶ’
   820вҶ’### 8.1 Reflection Prompt Generation
   821вҶ’
   822вҶ’**System prompt template:**
   823вҶ’```
   824вҶ’You are the reflection engine for Compass, a student development platform.
   825вҶ’
   826вҶ’Your job is to generate 2-3 personalized reflection questions for a high school student
   827вҶ’based on their history and recent activity.
   828вҶ’
   829вҶ’Rules:
   830вҶ’- Never prescribe direction. Only ask questions that help the student see their own patterns.
   831вҶ’- Never reference grades, rankings, or college admissions directly.
   832вҶ’- Questions should be warm, curious, and non-judgmental.
   833вҶ’- At least one question should connect two seemingly unrelated interests or activities.
   834вҶ’- At least one question should gently expand the student's awareness of something new.
   835вҶ’- Questions should be open-ended but specific enough to prompt real thought (not "how are you feeling?").
   836вҶ’- Keep questions under 30 words each.
   837вҶ’- Never repeat a question the student has been asked before.
   838вҶ’
   839вҶ’Student compressed profile:
   840вҶ’{compressed_summary}
   841вҶ’
   842вҶ’Recent activities (last 30 days):
   843вҶ’{recent_activities}
   844вҶ’
   845вҶ’Recent reflections (last 2 sessions):
   846вҶ’{recent_reflections}
   847вҶ’
   848вҶ’Previously asked questions (avoid repeating):
   849вҶ’{previous_prompts}
   850вҶ’
   851вҶ’Generate 2-3 reflection questions.
   852вҶ’```
   853вҶ’
   854вҶ’### 8.2 Signal Synthesis
   855вҶ’
   856вҶ’**System prompt template:**
   857вҶ’```
   858вҶ’You are the signal analysis engine for Compass.
   859вҶ’
   860вҶ’Analyze the student's complete activity and reflection history to identify:
   861вҶ’1. Interest clusters вҖ” groups of related interests with strength scores (0-1)
   862вҶ’2. Character signals вҖ” traits evidenced by behavior patterns
   863вҶ’3. Trajectory shifts вҖ” how interests have evolved over time
   864вҶ’4. Exploration gaps вҖ” areas aligned with their signals they haven't explored
   865вҶ’5. A compressed natural language summary (under 400 words) capturing who this student is becoming
   866вҶ’
   867вҶ’Rules:
   868вҶ’- Base all analysis on evidence (specific activities, reflections, patterns)
   869вҶ’- Never project or assume. If evidence is weak, say so.
   870вҶ’- Identify non-obvious connections between interests
   871вҶ’- Note when intensity is high but direction is unclear (this is normal and valuable)
   872вҶ’- Frame everything in terms of "emerging" and "evolving" вҖ” never as fixed traits
   873вҶ’
   874вҶ’Full student history:
   875вҶ’{full_activity_history}
   876вҶ’{full_reflection_history}
   877вҶ’
   878вҶ’Previous signal profile (if exists):
   879вҶ’{previous_profile}
   880вҶ’
   881вҶ’Generate the updated signal profile as JSON matching the signal_profiles schema.
   882вҶ’```
