     1‚Üí     1‚Üí# COMPASS ‚Äî Product Design Requirements (PDR)
     2‚Üí     2‚Üí## A Student Operating System for Self-Authorship
     3‚Üí     3‚Üí
     4‚Üí     4‚Üí---
     5‚Üí     5‚Üí
     6‚Üí     6‚Üí## 1. PRODUCT VISION
     7‚Üí     7‚Üí
     8‚Üí     8‚Üí### 1.1 One-Line Summary
     9‚Üí     9‚ÜíCompass is a longitudinal student operating system that turns raw high school experience into directional clarity ‚Äî not by telling students what to do, but by making their own signals visible and expanding their awareness of what's possible.
    10‚Üí    10‚Üí
    11‚Üí    11‚Üí### 1.2 Core Philosophy
    12‚Üí    12‚ÜíThe product is built on five principles derived from real student experience:
    13‚Üí    13‚Üí
    14‚Üí    14‚Üí1. **Neuroplasticity as foundation** ‚Äî Identity is a variable, not a constant. The system treats students as becoming, not being.
    15‚Üí    15‚Üí2. **Read, apply, compound** ‚Äî Curiosity without application is trivia. The system connects intellectual interest to real-world experimentation.
    16‚Üí    16‚Üí3. **Discipline shapes identity** ‚Äî Character formation (focus, endurance, frustration tolerance) matters as much as intellectual preference.
    17‚Üí    17‚Üí4. **Intrinsic motivation over external validation** ‚Äî The system never optimizes for prestige. It optimizes for engagement and growth.
    18‚Üí    18‚Üí5. **Community as accelerant** ‚Äî No one keeps you accountable like a peer. The system connects students in exploration, not competition.
    19‚Üí    19‚Üí
    20‚Üí    20‚Üí### 1.3 The Problem
    21‚Üí    21‚ÜíHigh school students suffer from **directional blindness**, not lack of ability. They generate signals constantly (books they read, classes they enjoy, activities they choose) but no system helps them see the pattern. By the time they discover career paths, major combinations, and industry pipelines, years have been wasted in reactive prestige-chasing.
    22‚Üí    22‚Üí
    23‚Üí    23‚ÜíCurrent tools (Naviance, Scoir, CollegeBoard) extract information from students for institutional purposes. Compass inverts this: everything belongs to the student first.
    24‚Üí    24‚Üí
    25‚Üí    25‚Üí### 1.4 The Solution
    26‚Üí    26‚ÜíA **reflection engine** ‚Äî not a direction engine. It functions as a mirror with memory:
    27‚Üí    27‚Üí- Tracks what students are actually doing and thinking
    28‚Üí    28‚Üí- Surfaces patterns they don't consciously see
    29‚Üí    29‚Üí- Expands their awareness of career paths, programs, and opportunities
    30‚Üí    30‚Üí- Never prescribes direction ‚Äî only reveals the map
    31‚Üí    31‚Üí
    32‚Üí    32‚Üí### 1.5 What Compass Is NOT
    33‚Üí    33‚Üí- Not an admissions optimizer
    34‚Üí    34‚Üí- Not a career quiz
    35‚Üí    35‚Üí- Not an AI essay writer
    36‚Üí    36‚Üí- Not a college ranking tool
    37‚Üí    37‚Üí- Not a ChatGPT wrapper
    38‚Üí    38‚Üí- Not a surveillance tool for schools or parents
    39‚Üí    39‚Üí
    40‚Üí    40‚Üí---

    42‚Üí    42‚Üí## 2. USER PERSONAS
    43‚Üí    43‚Üí
    44‚Üí    44‚Üí### 2.1 Primary: The Intense Drifter (Student)
    45‚Üí    45‚Üí- High school student (grades 9‚Äì12), ages 14‚Äì18
    46‚Üí    46‚Üí- Motivated and hardworking but lacks directional clarity
    47‚Üí    47‚Üí- Doesn't know what industries exist, what majors combine into, or what career architectures look like
    48‚Üí    48‚Üí- Generates strong signals through their choices but can't see the pattern
    49‚Üí    49‚Üí- Needs map visibility, not motivation
    50‚Üí    50‚Üí
    51‚Üí    51‚Üí### 2.2 Secondary: The Overwhelmed Counselor
    52‚Üí    52‚Üí- School guidance counselor managing 300‚Äì500+ students
    53‚Üí    53‚Üí- Meets each student 1-3 times per year
    54‚Üí    54‚Üí- Knows almost nothing about most students' evolving interests
    55‚Üí    55‚Üí- Needs a synthesized dashboard showing each student's trajectory
    56‚Üí    56‚Üí- Does NOT need raw data ‚Äî needs patterns and gaps
    57‚Üí    57‚Üí
    58‚Üí    58‚Üí### 2.3 Tertiary: The Anxious Parent (Future Phase)
    59‚Üí    59‚Üí- Wants reassurance their child is on track
    60‚Üí    60‚Üí- Currently spends money on admissions consultants out of fear
    61‚Üí    61‚Üí- Needs a "peace of mind" view ‚Äî not surveillance
    62‚Üí    62‚Üí- Only sees what the student explicitly shares
    63‚Üí    63‚Üí

    66‚Üí    66‚Üí## 3. CORE FEATURES
    67‚Üí    67‚Üí
    68‚Üí    68‚Üí### 3.1 Monthly Structured Reflection (School Integration)
    69‚Üí    69‚ÜíThe primary data input mechanism. Designed to be used during existing advisory/homeroom periods (~20 minutes/month).
    70‚Üí    70‚Üí
    71‚Üí    71‚Üí**Session Flow:**
    72‚Üí    72‚Üí1. **Check-in pulse** ‚Äî Quick emotional/energy state (not a mood tracker ‚Äî a context setter)
    73‚Üí    73‚Üí2. **Activity log update** ‚Äî What have you been doing this month? Classes, clubs, books, projects, hobbies, jobs, volunteer work. Semi-structured input with smart suggestions based on prior entries.
    74‚Üí    74‚Üí3. **Reflection prompts** ‚Äî 2-3 AI-generated questions personalized to the student's trajectory. These are the core product differentiator. Examples:
    75‚Üí    75‚Üí   - "You've spent time on both debate and coding this semester. What do you think connects those two interests?"
    76‚Üí    76‚Üí   - "Last month you said you were curious about how businesses work. Have you explored that further, or has your curiosity shifted?"
    77‚Üí    77‚Üí   - "You mentioned feeling energized by your chemistry lab. What specifically about it felt alive?"
    78‚Üí    78‚Üí4. **Map moment** ‚Äî After reflection, the system surfaces 1-2 "did you know?" expansions: career paths, programs, competitions, or industries related to the student's emerging signals.
    79‚Üí    79‚Üí5. **Privacy review** ‚Äî Student sees a summary of what would be visible to their counselor and toggles individual items on/off before submitting.
    80‚Üí    80‚Üí
    81‚Üí    81‚Üí**Data captured per session:**
    82‚Üí    82‚Üí- Activities (structured: category, time invested, enjoyment level)
    83‚Üí    83‚Üí- Free-text reflections
    84‚Üí    84‚Üí- Prompted response data
    85‚Üí    85‚Üí- Privacy selections
    86‚Üí    86‚Üí- Engagement metrics (time spent, depth of responses)
    87‚Üí    87‚Üí
    88‚Üí    88‚Üí### 3.2 Signal Mining Engine (AI Backend)
    89‚Üí    89‚ÜíThe intelligence layer that identifies patterns across a student's longitudinal data.
    90‚Üí    90‚Üí
    91‚Üí    91‚Üí**Pattern Detection:**
    92‚Üí    92‚Üí- Interest clustering ‚Äî groups activities and reflections into emerging theme areas
    93‚Üí    93‚Üí- Intensity tracking ‚Äî measures depth of engagement vs. surface-level participation
    94‚Üí    94‚Üí- Trajectory shifts ‚Äî detects when interests evolve, merge, or diverge over time
    95‚Üí    95‚Üí- Character signals ‚Äî identifies traits like discipline, leadership, creativity, analytical thinking based on activity patterns and reflections
    96‚Üí    96‚Üí- Gap detection ‚Äî flags areas the student hasn't explored that align with their emerging patterns
    97‚Üí    97‚Üí
    98‚Üí    98‚Üí**Signal Output (used by other features):**
    99‚Üí    99‚Üí- Evolving interest profile (updated monthly)
   100‚Üí   100‚Üí- Exploration breadth score (how widely has the student looked?)
   101‚Üí   101‚Üí- Depth indicators per interest area
   102‚Üí   102‚Üí- Confidence markers (how strong is each signal?)
   103‚Üí   103‚Üí- Suggested exploration prompts for the next month
   104‚Üí   104‚Üí
   105‚Üí   105‚Üí### 3.3 The Map (Career/Education Landscape)
   106‚Üí   106‚ÜíA living, personalized visualization of possibilities. NOT a static database ‚Äî a dynamic landscape that shifts as the student evolves.
   107‚Üí   107‚Üí
   108‚Üí   108‚Üí**Map Contents:**
   109‚Üí   109‚Üí- Career paths and their actual architectures (entry points, progressions, pivots)
   110‚Üí   110‚Üí- College major combinations and what they enable
   111‚Üí   111‚Üí- Industry pipelines (which schools feed which industries, which majors lead where)
   112‚Üí   112‚Üí- Recruiting patterns (target schools, typical paths, timeline)
   113‚Üí   113‚Üí- Competitions, research programs, internships, and experiential opportunities
   114‚Üí   114‚Üí- Double/triple major viability at specific schools
   115‚Üí   115‚Üí- Non-obvious connections (e.g., philosophy + systems engineering + consulting)
   116‚Üí   116‚Üí
   117‚Üí   117‚Üí**Personalization:**
   118‚Üí   118‚Üí- The visible portion of the map highlights areas relevant to the student's signals
   119‚Üí   119‚Üí- New regions illuminate as new signals emerge
   120‚Üí   120‚Üí- Previously explored areas show "you've been here" indicators
   121‚Üí   121‚Üí- Unexplored adjacent areas glow as suggested expansions
   122‚Üí   122‚Üí- The map never hides information ‚Äî students can always browse the full landscape
   123‚Üí   123‚Üí
   124‚Üí   124‚Üí**Data Sources (to be built and maintained):**
   125‚Üí   125‚Üí- BLS occupational data
   126‚Üí   126‚Üí- University program catalogs
   127‚Üí   127‚Üí- LinkedIn career path data (aggregated/anonymized)
   128‚Üí   128‚Üí- Competition and program databases
   129‚Üí   129‚Üí- Recruiting pipeline data from public sources
   130‚Üí   130‚Üí- Alumni outcome data where available
   131‚Üí   131‚Üí
   132‚Üí   132‚Üí### 3.4 Proactive Notification System
   133‚Üí   133‚ÜíThe key differentiator from a ChatGPT wrapper. The system comes to the student with the right question or opportunity at the right time.
   134‚Üí   134‚Üí
   135‚Üí   135‚Üí**Notification Types:**
   136‚Üí   136‚Üí
   137‚Üí   137‚Üí1. **Reflection Nudges** ‚Äî Between monthly sessions, contextual prompts based on recent activity or upcoming decisions.
   138‚Üí   138‚Üí   - "You registered for AP Physics next semester. What drew you to that choice?"
   139‚Üí   139‚Üí   - "It's been 3 months since you mentioned interest in writing. Still curious, or has that shifted?"
   140‚Üí   140‚Üí
   141‚Üí   141‚Üí2. **Opportunity Alerts** ‚Äî Timely, relevant opportunities the student wouldn't have found on their own.
   142‚Üí   142‚Üí   - "There's a philosophy essay competition with a deadline in 3 weeks. Based on your reflections, you might find the prompt interesting."
   143‚Üí   143‚Üí   - "MIT has a free online course on systems thinking that starts next month."
   144‚Üí   144‚Üí
   145‚Üí   145‚Üí3. **Map Expansions** ‚Äî "Did you know?" moments that widen peripheral vision.
   146‚Üí   146‚Üí   - "Students who enjoy both math and philosophy often thrive in operations research. Here's what that field looks like."
   147‚Üí   147‚Üí   - "UVA has a Systems Engineering program that combines technical and strategic thinking ‚Äî and it's a pipeline to consulting."
   148‚Üí   148‚Üí
   149‚Üí   149‚Üí4. **Peer Connection Prompts** ‚Äî Prompts to engage with matched exploration peers (see 3.6).
   150‚Üí   150‚Üí
   151‚Üí   151‚Üí**Notification Rules:**
   152‚Üí   152‚Üí- Maximum 2-3 notifications per week (quality over quantity)
   153‚Üí   153‚Üí- Every notification must be either a reflection prompt, a map expansion, or a timely opportunity
   154‚Üí   154‚Üí- Notifications are never generic ‚Äî always personalized to the student's trajectory
   155‚Üí   155‚Üí- Student controls notification frequency and can mute categories
   156‚Üí   156‚Üí- No notification should feel like marketing
   157‚Üí   157‚Üí
   158‚Üí   158‚Üí### 3.5 Counselor Dashboard
   159‚Üí   159‚ÜíA synthesized view of student trajectories ‚Äî NOT raw data.
   160‚Üí   160‚Üí
   161‚Üí   161‚Üí**What counselors see (by default):**
   162‚Üí   162‚Üí- Student's top 3-5 emerging interest areas with confidence levels
   163‚Üí   163‚Üí- Exploration breadth score
   164‚Üí   164‚Üí- Recent trajectory shifts ("This student's interests have shifted from X toward Y")
   165‚Üí   165‚Üí- Engagement level (is the student actively reflecting or disengaged?)
   166‚Üí   166‚Üí- Suggested conversation starters for the next 1:1 meeting
   167‚Üí   167‚Üí- Flagged gaps ("This student hasn't explored anything related to their strongest signals")
   168‚Üí   168‚Üí
   169‚Üí   169‚Üí**What counselors do NOT see (unless student shares):**
   170‚Üí   170‚Üí- Raw reflection text
   171‚Üí   171‚Üí- Specific books read or content consumed
   172‚Üí   172‚Üí- Emotional check-in data
   173‚Üí   173‚Üí- Any item the student has toggled off
   174‚Üí   174‚Üí
   175‚Üí   175‚Üí**Counselor Tools:**
   176‚Üí   176‚Üí- Filter/sort students by engagement level, interest area, or grade
   177‚Üí   177‚Üí- Batch view for identifying students who need attention
   178‚Üí   178‚Üí- Meeting prep view ‚Äî one-page summary per student before a scheduled meeting
   179‚Üí   179‚Üí- Ability to send a personalized reflection prompt to a specific student
   180‚Üí   180‚Üí
   181‚Üí   181‚Üí### 3.6 Peer Exploration Network
   182‚Üí   182‚ÜíLightweight social layer focused on shared exploration, not competition.
   183‚Üí   183‚Üí
   184‚Üí   184‚Üí**Matching Logic:**
   185‚Üí   185‚Üí- Students matched into small groups (3-5) based on similar exploration phase or overlapping interests
   186‚Üí   186‚Üí- NOT based on GPA, test scores, or achievement level
   187‚Üí   187‚Üí- Can be cross-school (for broader perspective) or within-school (for accountability)
   188‚Üí   188‚Üí
   189‚Üí   189‚Üí**Interaction Model:**
   190‚Üí   190‚Üí- Anonymized or first-name-only profiles
   191‚Üí   191‚Üí- Shared reflection prompts that the group responds to
   192‚Üí   192‚Üí- "Someone in your group discovered X this month" ‚Äî inspiration without comparison
   193‚Üí   193‚Üí- No rankings, no metrics visible to peers
   194‚Üí   194‚Üí- Opt-in only
   195‚Üí   195‚Üí
   196‚Üí   196‚Üí### 3.7 Future Simulation (Phase 2)
   197‚Üí   197‚ÜíShows possible trajectories based on the student's emerging signals.
   198‚Üí   198‚Üí
   199‚Üí   199‚Üí**Not prediction ‚Äî illustration:**
   200‚Üí   200‚Üí- "If you lean into engineering + philosophy, here's what 10 years might look like"
   201‚Üí   201‚Üí- Shows 3-5 possible paths based on current signals
   202‚Üí   202‚Üí- Each path shows: typical education route, career progression, skills developed, lifestyle characteristics
   203‚Üí   203‚Üí- Updates as signals evolve
   204‚Üí   204‚Üí- Explicitly framed as possibilities, not prescriptions
   205‚Üí   205‚Üí
   206‚Üí   206‚Üí### 3.8 Student Privacy Control Center
   207‚Üí   207‚ÜíThe trust architecture is a core product feature, not a compliance checkbox.
   208‚Üí   208‚Üí
   209‚Üí   209‚Üí**Privacy Principles:**
   210‚Üí   210‚Üí- Everything belongs to the student first
   211‚Üí   211‚Üí- Default state: private (student opts IN to sharing, not out)
   212‚Üí   212‚Üí- Granular control: per-item toggling after every session
   213‚Üí   213‚Üí- Audit log: student can always see exactly what their counselor has access to
   214‚Üí   214‚Üí- Data portability: student can export their entire history at any time
   215‚Üí   215‚Üí- Deletion rights: student can delete any data at any time
   216‚Üí   216‚Üí
   217‚Üí   217‚Üí**Privacy Dashboard:**
   218‚Üí   218‚Üí- Real-time view of what the counselor can currently see
   219‚Üí   219‚Üí- Toggle controls for each shared item
   220‚Üí   220‚Üí- History of what was shared and when
   221‚Üí   221‚Üí- One-click "make everything private" emergency option
   222‚Üí   222‚Üí

   227‚Üí   227‚Üí### 4.1 Design Philosophy: Glass Clarity

   232‚Üí   232‚Üí- Transparency as literal design metaphor: frosted glass panels, subtle translucency, light refraction effects
   233‚Üí   233‚Üí- High contrast content on soft, luminous backgrounds
   234‚Üí   234‚Üí- The premium feel signals to students: "this system takes you seriously"

   481‚Üí   481‚Üí- Tiered model routing: lightweight model for notifications/simple queries, powerful model for deep reflections and synthesis
   482‚Üí   482‚Üí
   483‚Üí   483‚Üí**Infrastructure:**
   484‚Üí   484‚Üí- Vercel or AWS (hosting)
   485‚Üí   485‚Üí- Firebase Cloud Messaging or OneSignal (push notifications)
   486‚Üí   486‚Üí- S3/CloudFlare R2 (file storage if needed)
   487‚Üí   487‚Üí- HIPAA/FERPA-compliant infrastructure (student data protection)
   488‚Üí   488‚Üí
   489‚Üí   489‚Üí### 5.3 AI Cost Architecture
   490‚Üí   490‚Üí
   491‚Üí   491‚Üí**Per-student monthly budget target: $2‚Äì5**
   492‚Üí   492‚Üí
   493‚Üí   493‚Üí**Cost control strategies:**
   494‚Üí   494‚Üí
   495‚Üí   495‚Üí1. **RAG over full context** ‚Äî Never stuff full student history into every call. Store all data in PostgreSQL + vector store. Retrieve only relevant slices per interaction (3‚Äì8k tokens instead of 50‚Äì100k).
   496‚Üí   496‚Üí
   497‚Üí   497‚Üí2. **Compressed student profile** ‚Äî A living summary document (1‚Äì2k tokens) updated monthly after each session. This travels with every interaction as base context.
   498‚Üí   498‚Üí
   499‚Üí   499‚Üí3. **Tiered model routing:**
   500‚Üí   500‚Üí   - Routine notifications and opportunity alerts ‚Üí lightweight model (Claude Haiku tier, ~$0.01/interaction)
   501‚Üí   501‚Üí   - Monthly reflection prompt generation ‚Üí mid-tier model (~$0.03/interaction)
   502‚Üí   502‚Üí   - Deep signal synthesis and profile updates ‚Üí top-tier model (~$0.05‚Äì0.08/interaction)
   503‚Üí   503‚Üí   - Estimated 80% of interactions use the cheap tier
   504‚Üí   504‚Üí
   505‚Üí   505‚Üí4. **Batch processing** ‚Äî Signal mining and profile synthesis run as batch jobs after monthly sessions, not real-time. Opportunity matching runs as a nightly cron against the full student database.
   506‚Üí   506‚Üí
   507‚Üí   507‚Üí5. **Structured interaction model** ‚Äî Prompted reflections with specific questions (not open-ended chat) naturally constrain token usage while producing better data.
   508‚Üí   508‚Üí
   509‚Üí   509‚Üí**Cost breakdown per student per month (estimated):**
   510‚Üí   510‚Üí| Component | Interactions | Cost/Interaction | Monthly Cost |
   511‚Üí   511‚Üí|---|---|---|---|
   512‚Üí   512‚Üí| Monthly session prompts | 3‚Äì5 | $0.03‚Äì0.05 | $0.15‚Äì0.25 |
   513‚Üí   513‚Üí| Signal synthesis (batch) | 1 | $0.08‚Äì0.15 | $0.08‚Äì0.15 |
   514‚Üí   514‚Üí| Profile update (batch) | 1 | $0.05‚Äì0.10 | $0.05‚Äì0.10 |
   515‚Üí   515‚Üí| Notifications (2‚Äì3/week) | 8‚Äì12 | $0.01‚Äì0.02 | $0.08‚Äì0.24 |
   516‚Üí   516‚Üí| Opportunity matching (batch) | 4 (weekly) | $0.02‚Äì0.05 | $0.08‚Äì0.20 |
   517‚Üí   517‚Üí| **Subtotal AI costs** | | | **$0.44‚Äì0.94** |
   518‚Üí   518‚Üí| Infrastructure/DB/hosting (amortized) | | | $1.00‚Äì2.00 |
   519‚Üí   519‚Üí| **Total per student/month** | | | **$1.44‚Äì2.94** |

   820‚Üí   820‚Üí### 8.1 Reflection Prompt Generation
   821‚Üí   821‚Üí
   822‚Üí   822‚Üí**System prompt template:**
   823‚Üí   823‚Üí```
   824‚Üí   824‚ÜíYou are the reflection engine for Compass, a student development platform.
   825‚Üí   825‚Üí
   826‚Üí   826‚ÜíYour job is to generate 2-3 personalized reflection questions for a high school student
   827‚Üí   827‚Üíbased on their history and recent activity.

   829‚Üí   829‚ÜíRules:
   830‚Üí   830‚Üí- Never prescribe direction. Only ask questions that help the student see their own patterns.
   831‚Üí   831‚Üí- Never reference grades, rankings, or college admissions directly.
   832‚Üí   832‚Üí- Questions should be warm, curious, and non-judgmental.
   833‚Üí   833‚Üí- At least one question should connect two seemingly unrelated interests or activities.
   834‚Üí   834‚Üí- At least one question should gently expand the student's awareness of something new.
   835‚Üí   835‚Üí- Questions should be open-ended but specific enough to prompt real thought (not "how are you feeling?").
   836‚Üí   836‚Üí- Keep questions under 30 words each.
   837‚Üí   837‚Üí- Never repeat a question the student has been asked before.
   838‚Üí   838‚Üí
   839‚Üí   839‚ÜíStudent compressed profile:
   840‚Üí   840‚Üí{compressed_summary}
   841‚Üí   841‚Üí
   842‚Üí   842‚ÜíRecent activities (last 30 days):
   843‚Üí   843‚Üí{recent_activities}
   844‚Üí   844‚Üí
   845‚Üí   845‚ÜíRecent reflections (last 2 sessions):
   846‚Üí   846‚Üí{recent_reflections}
   847‚Üí   847‚Üí
   848‚Üí   848‚ÜíPreviously asked questions (avoid repeating):
   849‚Üí   849‚Üí{previous_prompts}
   850‚Üí   850‚Üí
   851‚Üí   851‚ÜíGenerate 2-3 reflection questions.
   852‚Üí   852‚Üí```

   855‚Üí   855‚Üí

   857‚Üí   857‚Üí```
   858‚Üí   858‚ÜíYou are the signal analysis engine for Compass.
   859‚Üí   859‚Üí
   860‚Üí   860‚ÜíAnalyze the student's complete activity and reflection history to identify:
   861‚Üí   861‚Üí1. Interest clusters ‚Äî groups of related interests with strength scores (0-1)
   862‚Üí   862‚Üí2. Character signals ‚Äî traits evidenced by behavior patterns
   863‚Üí   863‚Üí3. Trajectory shifts ‚Äî how interests have evolved over time
   864‚Üí   864‚Üí4. Exploration gaps ‚Äî areas aligned with their signals they haven't explored

   866‚Üí   866‚Üí
   867‚Üí   867‚ÜíRules:
   868‚Üí   868‚Üí- Base all analysis on evidence (specific activities, reflections, patterns)

   870‚Üí   870‚Üí- Identify non-obvious connections between interests
   871‚Üí   871‚Üí- Note when intensity is high but direction is unclear (this is normal and valuable)

   873‚Üí   873‚Üí
   874‚Üí   874‚ÜíFull student history:
   875‚Üí   875‚Üí{full_activity_history}
   876‚Üí   876‚Üí{full_reflection_history}
   877‚Üí   877‚Üí
   878‚Üí   878‚ÜíPrevious signal profile (if exists):
   879‚Üí   879‚Üí{previous_profile}
   880‚Üí   880‚Üí
   881‚Üí   881‚ÜíGenerate the updated signal profile as JSON matching the signal_profiles schema.
   882‚Üí   882‚Üí```
